THE INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION (1750-1950)
Sulpakan


Inventor/Creator:
José Rizal invented the Sulpakan (also spelled sulpukan), a unique wood-based cigarette lighter, showcasing his creativity beyond medicine and literature.


When was it made:
The Sulpakan was created in 1887 during the late Industrial Revolution period, while Rizal was actively practicing medicine and engaging in scientific work.


Location:
It was developed in Calamba, Laguna, Philippines, where Rizal lived and worked before his travels abroad.


Purpose:
The Sulpakan served as a functional fire piston or lighter designed for lighting cigarettes. It was also gifted as a token of friendship and to demonstrate inventive skill.


What was the use of it:
It was used as a lighter, producing fire through friction or mechanical compression, and also served as a demonstration of Rizal’s scientific and inventive abilities.


How to use it:
To use the Sulpakan, a small piece of tinder is placed inside the chamber, then the piston is quickly and forcefully pressed downward. This rapid motion creates heat that ignites the tinder, producing a flame.


How it works:
The Sulpakan works as a fire piston using air compression. When the piston is rapidly pushed into a sealed cylinder, the air inside is compressed, causing a sharp rise in temperature that ignites the tinder and produces fire without matches.


Significance:
The invention highlights José Rizal’s talents as an inventor and scientist beyond his literary works. The Sulpakan demonstrates early Filipino innovation in mechanical fire-starting technology.
