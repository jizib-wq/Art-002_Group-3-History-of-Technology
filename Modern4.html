MODERN TECHNOLOGY (2010s-PRESENT)
Salamander Amphibious Tricycle (Disaster-Resilient Transport Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
H2O Technologies designed the Salamander Amphibious Tricycle, a hybrid vehicle built for flood-prone areas in the Philippines.


When was it made:
It was developed in the 2010s in response to increasing flooding caused by typhoons and climate change.


Location:
The invention was created in the Philippines, where flooding is a major transportation challenge in urban and rural areas.


Purpose:
The purpose of the Salamander was to provide a reliable transport system that can operate both on land and in floodwaters.


What was the use of it:
It was used to transport passengers and rescue individuals during floods, while also serving as a regular tricycle during normal conditions.


How to use it:
The driver operates it like a normal tricycle on land. When flooding occurs, the vehicle transitions into water mode, allowing it to float and move through flooded streets for transport or rescue operations.


How it works:
The Salamander works through a hybrid design that combines wheels for land travel and buoyant hull structures for water travel. It may use a propeller or water jet system for movement in floods, while its sealed body prevents water from entering critical mechanical parts.


Significance:
The Salamander Amphibious Tricycle is significant because it provides an innovative solution to flooding problems in the Philippines. It improves disaster response, enhances mobility during emergencies, and shows how Filipino engineering can adapt transportation technology to climate-related challenges.
