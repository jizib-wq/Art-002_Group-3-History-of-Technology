Prehistoric & Ancient Technology (3.4M YEARS AGO-500AD)
Callao Man Tools (Early Human Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
Tools associated with Callao Man were used by early human species that lived in the Philippines over 50,000–67,000 years ago.


When was it made:
These tools and remains date back to approximately 67,000 years ago during the Late Pleistocene period.


Location:
They were discovered in Callao Cave, Peñablanca, Cagayan Province in northern Luzon.


Purpose:
These early tools were used for survival activities such as hunting, food preparation, and tool-making.


What was the use of it:
They helped early humans process food, build shelters, and adapt to their environment through basic technology.


How to use it:
To use these tools, early humans would hold sharpened stones or bone implements and apply them to specific tasks such as cutting meat, scraping animal skins, or shaping wood.


How it works:
These tools work through their physical properties—sharp edges for cutting and sturdy materials for breaking or shaping.


Significance:
Callao Man tools are important evidence of early human life and intelligence in the Philippines.
