THE DIGITAL AGE (1950-PRESENT)
Make-roscope


Inventor/Creator:
The Make-roscope is an innovation invented by Jeremy De Leon from the Philippines. It was created due to the lack of adequate microscopes for students during classroom sessions.


When was it made:
The Make-roscope was developed in the modern Digital Age, around 2021, as part of efforts to improve science education in the Philippines.


Location:
It was created in Quezon City, Philippines, where limited access to laboratory equipment inspired the invention.


Purpose:
The Make-roscope is designed to enhance science education in schools through an affordable microscope that students can use anytime.


What was the use of it:
It is a compact portable microscope that can be attached to a smartphone, allowing students to view small objects for scientific experiments.


How to use it:
The Make-roscope is attached to a smartphone camera lens. A small object such as a leaf, fabric, or insect sample is placed under the lens. By opening the camera or microscope app, the user can view the magnified image on the phone screen.


How it works:
It works by using optical magnification combined with a smartphone camera. The lens enlarges tiny objects while the phone captures and displays the magnified image, allowing users to see microscopic details without expensive equipment.


Significance:
The Make-roscope is important because it makes science learning more accessible and affordable for students, especially in schools with limited resources. It promotes STEM education and encourages curiosity, innovation, and hands-on learning.
