THE MIDDLE AGES & RENAISSANCE (500-1750)
Balangay Boat (Advanced Maritime Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
The Balangay was built by skilled early Filipino seafarers from Austronesian communities, especially those living in coastal settlements such as Butuan.


When was it made:
The balangay dates back as early as 320 CE, but its use continued and developed through the Middle Ages and early colonial period up to around the 1500s.


Location:
It was widely used in coastal and riverine areas of the Philippines, with major archaeological discoveries found in Butuan, Mindanao.


Purpose:
The balangay was designed for long-distance sea travel, trade, and migration between islands.


What was the use of it:
It was used to transport people, goods, and cultural practices, helping early Filipinos build strong maritime trade networks.


How to use it:
To use the balangay, trained rowers and navigators would coordinate paddling while a leader guided direction using knowledge of stars, winds, and currents.


How it works:
The balangay works through buoyancy and human or wind-powered movement, using wooden planks joined without nails for flexibility.


Significance:
The balangay is a symbol of early Filipino innovation and seafaring skill, and the origin of the word “barangay.”
