Karaoke Machine (Entertainment Technology Innovation)


Inventor/Creator:
Roberto del Rosario invented the “Sing-Along System,” which later became known as the karaoke machine.


When was it made:
The invention was patented in 1975, marking a major innovation in entertainment technology.


Location:
It was developed in the Philippines by Roberto del Rosario, who was based in Manila.


Purpose:
The purpose of the invention was to allow people to sing along to recorded music without a live band.


What was the use of it:
It was used for entertainment in homes, bars, and events, eventually becoming a global form of social singing known as karaoke.


How to use it:
To use the karaoke machine, a user selects a song, plays the instrumental track, and sings along while reading lyrics displayed on a screen. A microphone is used to amplify the voice, and some systems allow recording or scoring performance.


How it works:
The karaoke machine works by removing or lowering the original vocal track from a recorded song, leaving only the instrumental music. The system then plays the backing track while displaying synchronized lyrics so users can sing in time.


Significance:
The karaoke machine is a major contribution to global entertainment culture. It revolutionized social singing by making music participation accessible to everyone, not just professional singers. It also highlights Filipino innovation in the entertainment industry.
