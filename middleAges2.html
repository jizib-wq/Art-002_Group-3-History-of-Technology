THE MIDDLE AGES & RENAISSANCE (500-1750)
Traditional Weaving and Textile Technology


Inventor/Creator:
Early Filipino weavers, especially women in indigenous communities, developed traditional weaving tools such as the backstrap loom.


When was it made:
Weaving traditions existed long before colonization and continued strongly during the Middle Ages and early Spanish period (500–1700s).


Location:
Weaving practices were widespread in regions like the Cordillera, Mindanao, and Visayas.


Purpose:
The purpose of weaving was to create clothing, blankets, and ceremonial fabrics.


What was the use of it:
Woven textiles were used for clothing, trade, rituals, and as symbols of identity.


How to use it:
The weaver ties one end of the loom to a fixed object and the other around their waist, then controls tension while weaving threads into patterns.


How it works:
The loom works by interlacing warp and weft threads, using tension and wooden tools to create designs.


Significance:
Traditional weaving is a symbol of Filipino identity and culture, showing creativity and preserving traditions.
