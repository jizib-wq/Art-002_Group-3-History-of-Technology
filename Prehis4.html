Prehistoric & Ancient Technology (3.4M YEARS AGO-500AD)
Shell Tools and Ornamentation (Coastal Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
Early coastal communities in the Philippines created Shell Tools using shells from marine environments.


When was it made:
These tools date back to around 40,000–20,000 years ago during the Stone Age period.


Location:
Shell tools have been found in Palawan, especially in the Tabon Cave complex.


Purpose:
They were used for cutting, scraping, and crafting everyday materials.


What was the use of it:
Shell tools were also used for personal decoration, showing early cultural expression and craftsmanship.


How to use it:
To use shell tools, early people would hold sharpened shell edges and apply them to tasks like slicing plants, scraping animal hides, or carving materials. Smaller shells were also worn as decorative items.


How it works:
Shell tools work because of their naturally sharp edges and smooth surfaces. By breaking or shaping shells, early Filipinos created tools that could cut or scrape efficiently.


Significance:
Shell tools and ornaments show the adaptability and creativity of early Filipino coastal communities.
