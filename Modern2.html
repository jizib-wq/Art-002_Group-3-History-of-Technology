MODERN TECHNOLOGY (2010s-PRESENT)
RxBox Telehealth Diagnostic Device (Medical Technology Innovation)


Inventor/Creator:
The University of the Philippines developed the RxBox Telehealth Diagnostic Device in partnership with health and engineering researchers to improve medical access in remote communities.


When was it made:
The RxBox system was developed in the 2010s as part of government-supported health innovation programs.


Location:
It was created in the Philippines through research collaborations led by the University of the Philippines and deployed in rural health centers nationwide.


Purpose:
The purpose of RxBox was to provide affordable and accessible medical diagnostics for communities with limited healthcare facilities.


What was the use of it:
It was used to measure and transmit vital health data such as ECG, blood pressure, and pulse rate to doctors in real time, enabling remote diagnosis and treatment.


How to use it:
Healthcare workers attach sensors to a patient’s body to collect vital signs. The device displays the readings and sends the data electronically to doctors in another location for evaluation and medical advice.


How it works:
RxBox works using biomedical sensors connected to a digital system. These sensors detect and measure body signals such as heart rate and blood pressure, then convert them into digital signals transmitted through communication networks to healthcare professionals.


Significance:
RxBox is highly significant because it improves healthcare access in remote and underserved areas of the Philippines. It supports telemedicine, enables early disease detection, and helps reduce the gap between rural patients and urban medical specialists.
