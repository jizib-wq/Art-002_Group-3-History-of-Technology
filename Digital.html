THE DIGITAL AGE (1950-PRESENT)
Videophone (Early Video Communication Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
Gregorio Y. Zara invented an early videophone system known as a “photo phone signal separator network,” making him one of the pioneers of video communication technology.


When was it made:
The videophone was developed and patented in 1955 during the post-industrial technological expansion period.


Location:
It was developed in the Philippines, where Dr. Zara conducted his scientific research and engineering work.


Purpose:
The invention was designed to allow two-way communication where people could both see and talk to each other through a screen.


What was the use of it:
It was used as an early form of video calling technology, laying the foundation for modern video conferencing systems such as Zoom and mobile video calls.


How to use it:
Both users would connect through a device with a camera and display screen. A call would be initiated so that audio and visual signals could be transmitted simultaneously, allowing real-time communication.


How it works:
The videophone works by converting sound and images into electrical signals. The camera captures live video while the microphone captures voice. These signals are transmitted through communication lines and converted back into audio and images on the receiving device.


Significance:
The videophone is an important innovation in communication technology because it introduced face-to-face long-distance interaction. It helped pave the way for modern video communication tools used today in education, business, and personal communication.
