Prehistoric & Ancient Technology (3.4M YEARS AGO-500AD)
Stone Tools (Early Survival Technology)


Inventor/Creator:
The earliest Stone Tools were created by early human ancestors in the Philippines, including possible Homo erectus-like populations and early hunter-gatherer groups who lived thousands to millions of years ago.


When was it made:
These tools date back as early as 700,000–50,000 years ago during the Paleolithic period, making them some of the oldest known technologies in the country.


Location:
Stone tools have been discovered in areas such as Cagayan Valley in Luzon and Palawan in the western Philippines.


Purpose:
The purpose of stone tools was to help early humans survive by enabling them to hunt animals, process food, and defend themselves.


What was the use of it:
They were used for cutting meat, scraping hides, breaking bones, and shaping wood, making daily survival easier in prehistoric environments.


How to use it:
To use stone tools, early humans would grip the sharpened edge and apply force depending on the task.


How it works:
Stone tools work through their sharp edges and durable material. By striking one stone against another, early humans created flakes with sharp cutting edges.


Significance:
Stone tools are among the earliest signs of human intelligence and innovation in the Philippines.
